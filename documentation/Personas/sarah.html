Social Worker Sarah Jones


Technology

iPhone 10, Chrome as browser, Macbook Pro M2 processor, 16GB RAM, Chrome as the default browser. Uses Nikon D60 for photos. Mac Computer/iPhone 11 - iOS 16.2

Love && Hate

  • She helps clients find local fruit trees and gardens for free fresh produce.
  • She recommends gardening to clients for stress relief and anxiety reduction.
  • She hates that she doesn't have a lot of time for every one of her clients.

Attitude && Needs

  • Sarah's clients come from low-income families and may not have access to resources such as water or tools and the app provides that for them.
  • She knows her clients may not have the experience or knowledge necessary to grow fruit trees successfully and wants to help them.
  • She is very involved in the community and loves helping others.
Roles

Sarah is a long-time social worker with a strong commitment to helping communities find resources.

Use Case

Sarah is a social worker who uses the FruitTrees app to improve her clients' lives by finding resources for immediate needs and promoting community engagement, and environmental awareness.

Preconditions

Sarah is already logged onto the site.

Post-conditions

Sarah continued to use the site after she found what she needed to continue to help other clients.
  • Sarah visits the Fruit Tree website for the first time and registers for an account using their name and email/number information.
  • After registering Sarah gets a verification email. They click on the link to verify their account and get access to the website.
  • Sarah signs on and lands on the site's dashboard.
  • On the dashboard, Sarah sees a search bar and a map that displays fruit tree icons indicating potential resources. They enter their location or the location of a client they're assisting.
  • The site displays a list of fruit trees in the chosen area. Sarah can see details such as the type of fruit, the owner's contact information, and whether the tree owner is open to sharing their produce.
  • Sarah sees a few fruit trees that seem promising for their clients. They use the site's messaging feature to contact the tree owners, introducing themselves as a social worker and explaining what they want.
  • Once in contact with tree owners who are willing to share their fruit, Sarah sets times for harvesting. They coordinate with the tree owners to ensure a smooth and respectful process.
  • On the scheduled day, Sarah harvests the fruit from the identified trees. They organize the collected produce for distribution to the families in need within their community.
  • Sarah continues to use the website to locate fruit trees as needed to support their clients. They receive regular updates and notifications about new listings and community events related to fruit sharing.
  • When she's finished Sarah logs out of their account.
